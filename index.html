Predicting 4D Robot-Object Interactions by Distilling Pre-trained Video Models

A hybrid 4D Gaussian world model that distills video foundation priors into explicit geometry, physical contact, and directly executable robot trajectories.

Anonymous Submission
Method Results

Abstract

Predicting future states is central to embodied robot learning, but most existing approaches perform prediction in 2D pixel space through video generation, which discards explicit geometric structure and lacks physical consistency. While 4D prediction offers a more faithful representation of the physical world, current 4D generation methods are typically limited to single-object motion and do not directly produce executable robot trajectories, requiring an additional inverse dynamics module. We address these limitations by distilling a hybrid 4D representation from video foundation models. The hybrid 4D representation jointly models a deforming object and an articulated robot within a unified Gaussian Splatting framework. The representation is first optimized by distilling a pre-trained video diffusion model to generate plausible object deformation and robot motion, and then refined by differentiable trajectory optimization to produce physically plausible interactions and executable joint commands. Experiments show that our approach outperforms video and 4D prediction baselines in physical and geometric consistency, supports complex robot-object interactions, and naturally composes with new robots and environments.
