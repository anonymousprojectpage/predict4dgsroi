Abstract

Predicting future states is central to embodied robot learning, but most existing approaches predict in 2D pixel space through video generation, discarding explicit geometry and physical consistency. We distill a hybrid 4D representation from video foundation models that jointly models a deforming object and an articulated robot within a unified Gaussian Splatting framework.

The representation is first optimized by distilling Wan 2.2 to generate plausible object deformation and robot motion, then refined with reinforcement learning to enforce physically grounded contact and executable joint commands. Experiments show stronger physical and geometric consistency than video and 4D prediction baselines, while supporting composition with new robots and environments.

Motivation

Why Predict in 4D?

01

Executable Motion

Robot motion is optimized directly in 3D joint space, avoiding an extra inverse dynamics model after video generation.

02

Geometric Consistency

Scene and robot Gaussians are rendered together, preserving depth ordering, multi-view structure, and shared gradients.

03

Composability

Robots and environments remain separate dynamic 3D entities, allowing new embodiments or scenes to be plugged in efficiently.

Method

Hybrid 4D Gaussian World Model

A unified representation couples deformable object geometry with joint-driven robot Gaussians.

Input

Static Gaussian Scene

Mesh-extracted scene with Gaussian primitives anchored to mesh faces.

Stage 1

Video SDS Distillation

Wan 2.2 guides plausible object deformation and robot motion under geometric regularizers.

Stage 2

RL Motion Refinement

Contact rewards refine the joint trajectory for physical feasibility and smooth execution.

Hybrid Representation

Mesh-anchored scene Gaussians move through a sparse deformation graph, while link-attached robot Gaussians follow differentiable forward kinematics from joint configurations. Concatenating both sets into one rasterizer keeps visibility and gradients consistent between the robot and object.

Physical Refinement

After video-guided optimization, the 4D scene is frozen and the robot trajectory is refined with force-closure, contact-tracking, joint-limit, velocity, acceleration, and end-effector prior rewards.
